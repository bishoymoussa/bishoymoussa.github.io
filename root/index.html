About Me

Machine Learning Researcher

I'm a Machine Learning researcher and graduate student at Northeastern University, specializing in Computer Vision and Multi-Object Tracking. My research focuses on developing innovative tracking algorithms for complex scenarios using transformer-based architectures and graph neural networks.

Currently, I'm working on my Master's thesis under the guidance of Prof. Sarah Ostadabbas in the ACLab at Northeastern University, where I'm researching advanced methods for multi-subject tracking utilizing both single and multi-camera setups.

Research

Current Research Focus

My research is centered around improving multi-object tracking in complex scenarios. I'm particularly interested in developing transformer-enhanced graphical tracking algorithms that can handle occlusions, varying lighting conditions, and multiple camera views. My work combines deep learning techniques with graph-based approaches to achieve state-of-the-art tracking performance.

Research Areas

  • Transformer-enhanced graphical multi-person tracking
  • Multiple toddler tracking in indoor videos
  • Classification of infant sleep-wake states from video data
  • Machine learning models for cancer prediction and treatment optimization
  • Motion analysis for exoskeleton control
